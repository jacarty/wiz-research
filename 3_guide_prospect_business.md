# UK Customer Business Intelligence Research - User Guide

## What It Does

Conducts comprehensive business intelligence research on UK-headquartered companies for sales strategy development. Delivers verified, source-backed intelligence that sales and customer success teams can trust.

**Key Features:**
- Company foundation and financial profile
- Business strategy and current priorities
- Market position and competitive landscape
- Regulatory and compliance context
- Leadership team and organizational structure
- Business challenges and pain points
- Partnership ecosystem and customer presence
- **Verification-first methodology** - only includes information with sources

## What You'll Get

### 7 Core Sections (10-15 pages):

1. **Company Foundation**
   - Official profile and structure
   - Financial metrics (revenue, headcount, growth)
   - Recent milestones and developments

2. **Business Strategy & Priorities**
   - Stated strategic objectives from official sources
   - Business initiatives and investments
   - Priorities inferred from job posting patterns

3. **Market Position & Competitive Landscape**
   - Competitive positioning and market share
   - Recent competitive developments

4. **Regulatory & Compliance Context**
   - Industry regulations and requirements (FCA, ICO, sector-specific)
   - Certifications and audit requirements
   - Upcoming regulatory changes

5. **Leadership & Organization**
   - Executive leadership team profiles (CEO, CFO, CISO, CTO)
   - Organizational structure insights
   - Key functional leaders

6. **Business Challenges & Pain Points**
   - Stated challenges from official sources
   - Operational and growth obstacles
   - UK-specific challenges (Brexit, economic factors)

7. **Partnership & Ecosystem**
   - Strategic partnerships and alliances
   - Customer segments and market presence

### Plus: Executive Summary
- Quick facts and top priorities
- Key decision-makers with LinkedIn profiles
- Notable challenges and recent events
- Information gaps requiring discovery questions
- Research confidence assessment

## Key Differentiators

### Verification Standards (Critical)

**VERIFIED means:**
- Direct statement from official company source
- Public record (Companies House, ICO, FCA Register)
- Attributed statement in credible news with date
- Official announcement or partnership press release
- **Job posting patterns** (3+ postings showing consistent themes)

**NEVER includes:**
- Assumptions based on "industry norms"
- Speculation or "likely" statements without evidence
- Information without specific URL and date
- Single job posting as sole evidence

**If unverifiable:**
```
Status: NOT FOUND
Search conducted: [Where you looked]
Discovery question: [Question to ask during sales conversation]
```

### Output Format Standards

Every finding uses this structure:
```
**[Finding Title]**
- What we know: [Specific fact]
- Source: [Full URL]
- Date: [Publication/access date]
- Why it matters: [Relevance to sales strategy]
```

### Length Management
- **10-15 pages maximum** - quality over quantity
- **3-5 findings per subsection** - most relevant only
- **Recent information prioritized** - last 12-18 months
- **Skip empty sections** - don't pad with speculation

## Tips for Best Results

**1. Provide Accurate Starting Information**
- ✅ Correct company legal name and website URL
- ✅ Companies House number if known (saves time)
- ❌ Don't guess at URLs or assume subsidiary relationships

**2. Be Clear About Research Focus**
- "Focus particularly on cloud infrastructure priorities"
- "Emphasize security leadership and reporting structure"
- "Priority: understanding regulatory compliance burden"

**3. Specify Industry Context When Needed**
- "Company operates in regulated financial services"
- "B2B SaaS company serving enterprise customers"
- "Healthcare technology provider to NHS trusts"

**4. Request Additional Depth on Relevant Areas**
- "Expand the regulatory section with detailed compliance frameworks"
- "Include extra detail on technology leadership background"
- "Find more evidence of current security initiatives"

**5. Use for Pre-Call Preparation**
- Review executive summary before customer calls
- Use discovery questions for information gaps
- Reference verified challenges in sales conversations

## Quality Standards

### The Prompt Enforces:

**Source Requirements:**
- Every finding has specific URL with date
- No unsourced claims about company
- Job inferences require 3+ postings
- Companies House data verified
- LinkedIn profiles found for key executives

**Verification Standards:**
- Official sources preferred (company, regulatory filings)
- News articles must be credible with attribution
- Clear distinction between facts and patterns
- Transparent about information gaps

**Conciseness:**
- 10-15 pages maximum
- 3-5 key findings per subsection
- Recent information prioritized
- No padding or speculation

**UK-Specific:**
- Companies House records checked
- UK regulatory context identified
- Brexit implications considered where relevant
- UK business news sources reviewed

## When to Use This Prompt

**Ideal For:**
- UK-headquartered companies (uses UK-specific resources)
- B2B sales preparation and account planning
- Pre-meeting research requiring verified intelligence
- Building account strategies based on real priorities
- Understanding decision-makers and organizational structure

**Less Ideal For:**
- Non-UK companies (different official records and news sources)
- Consumer/B2C companies (less public business information)
- Very small companies (limited public information available)
- Companies requiring real-time news monitoring (point-in-time research)

**Complementary Research:**
- Combine with Industry Strategy Map for sector context
- Use with competitive intelligence on specific competitors
- Follow up with targeted technology stack analysis
- Supplement with social selling intelligence from LinkedIn
