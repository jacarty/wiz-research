# Call Summary & Action Report

Analyze this single call recording and extract actionable intelligence.

## Input
Audio/transcript of this call only (no additional context available)

## Output Structure

### 1. CALL OVERVIEW
- Date, participants (name, title if mentioned), meeting purpose
- Duration and overall tone/engagement level

### 2. KEY DISCUSSION POINTS
- Main topics covered in order discussed
- Problems/challenges raised by customer
- Questions asked by customer (signals interest areas)
- Wiz capabilities discussed or demoed
- Current tools/situation mentioned

### 3. ATTENDEE ANALYSIS
For each participant who spoke:
- Engagement: Active | Moderate | Passive
- Sentiment: Enthusiastic | Positive | Neutral | Skeptical | Resistant
- Key statements or questions they raised
- Confidence: High/Medium/Low

### 4. BUYING SIGNALS & CONCERNS
**Positive Indicators:**
- Urgency cues, timeline mentions, budget discussions
- Technical deep-dives or integration questions
- Next step proposals from customer side

**Concerns/Objections:**
- Hesitations or pushback expressed
- Competitor mentions
- Blockers or constraints raised

### 5. COMMITTED NEXT STEPS
- Actions agreed with owner and deadline
- What customer committed to do
- What we committed to provide
- Follow-up meeting scheduled (date/time/agenda)

### 6. RECOMMENDED FOLLOW-UP ACTIONS
- Immediate actions (within 24-48hrs)
- Information to research before next interaction
- Stakeholders not present who should be engaged
- Resources/content to prepare

### 7. INDIVIDUAL ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY
For each key participant:
- **Follow-up topic**: One specific area to explore with them based on their comments
- **Question set**: 3 targeted questions to uncover their priorities/concerns/influence

### 8. INFORMATION GAPS
What we still don't know that matters:
- Decision-making process/timeline
- Budget/authority signals
- Technical requirements/constraints
- Key stakeholders not on call
- Competitive landscape

## Quality Standards
- Quote directly from call (with approximate timestamp if possible)
- Mark assumptions as "Inferred" vs. stated facts
- Focus on what was actually said, not what should have been discussed
- Flag if critical sales qualification areas weren't covered
