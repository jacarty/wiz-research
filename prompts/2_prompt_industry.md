# Industry Strategy Map Generator Prompt
## Executive Intelligence Brief

Create an executive-level Industry Strategy Map for the [INDUSTRY] industry and `[SEGMENT]`.

**OUTPUT FORMAT: Deliver as a structured text document (Markdown format preferred).**

This should be a comprehensive but scannable intelligence brief that an executive or strategic professional would use to quickly understand an industry's dynamics, opportunities, and technology landscape.

Please structure the output as follows:

---

## 1. INDUSTRY OVERVIEW

**Market Landscape:**
- Current market size and projected growth (next 3-5 years)
- Key segments and sub-sectors
- Major players and market concentration
- Geographic considerations and regional variations

**Market Maturity:**
- Industry lifecycle stage (emerging, growth, mature, declining)
- Consolidation trends
- Innovation velocity

---

## 2. GROWTH AREAS & REVENUE DRIVERS

Identify the 4-6 primary areas where companies in this industry are finding growth and generating revenue:

**For each growth area:**
- **Description**: What is driving this growth opportunity
- **Market Size/Potential**: Quantify the opportunity where possible
- **Key Players**: Who is winning in this space
- **Timeline**: Is this near-term (0-2 years), mid-term (2-5 years), or long-term (5+ years)
- **Business Model**: How companies monetize this opportunity

---

## 3. STRATEGIC CHALLENGES & RISK FACTORS

**Operational Challenges:**
- List 4-6 key operational obstacles companies face
- Include cost pressures, resource constraints, execution challenges

**Market Challenges:**
- Competitive dynamics and threats
- Customer behavior shifts
- Economic headwinds or market saturation

**Technology Challenges:**
- Legacy system constraints
- Skills gaps and talent shortages
- Integration and modernization hurdles

**Strategic Risks:**
- Disruption threats
- Regulatory changes on the horizon
- Geopolitical or macroeconomic factors

---

## 4. REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE LANDSCAPE

**Core Frameworks:**
- List primary regulations and compliance standards (e.g., GDPR, SOC2, FCA, HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
- Explain applicability and scope

**Compliance Priorities:**
- Most critical compliance areas for this industry
- Common audit focus areas
- Penalties and enforcement trends

**Emerging Regulations:**
- New or upcoming regulatory changes
- How they will impact business operations
- Timeline for implementation

**Regional Variations:**
- Key differences by geography (if applicable)
- Cross-border considerations

---

## 5. TECHNOLOGY INNOVATION & DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION

**This is a critical section** - explain how technology is being leveraged to drive innovation, competitive advantage, and revenue growth.

### Cloud Adoption & Strategy
- **Current State**: Where is the industry in cloud adoption (early, mid, mature)
- **Cloud Usage Patterns**: IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS adoption
- **Strategic Drivers**: Why companies are moving to cloud (cost, scale, innovation, speed)
- **Cloud-Native Innovation**: How cloud enables new business models or capabilities
- **Multi-Cloud Trends**: Single cloud vs multi-cloud strategies
- **Examples**: Specific use cases or success stories

### SaaS & Platform Economics
- **SaaS Adoption**: Which business functions are moving to SaaS
- **Platform Strategies**: How companies are building or leveraging platforms
- **Integration Ecosystem**: Key SaaS integrations and workflows
- **ROI & Business Case**: How SaaS drives cost savings or revenue growth
- **Vendor Consolidation**: Trends toward integrated suites vs best-of-breed
- **Examples**: Leading SaaS products in this industry

### AI & Machine Learning Applications
- **Use Cases by Business Function**: Where AI is being applied
  - Revenue generation (personalization, recommendations, pricing)
  - Cost optimization (automation, efficiency, prediction)
  - Risk management (fraud detection, compliance, security)
  - Customer experience (chatbots, support, engagement)
- **AI Maturity**: Where is the industry in AI adoption (experimental, pilots, production, competitive advantage)
- **Infrastructure Requirements**: Data platforms, MLOps, compute needs
- **Competitive Dynamics**: Is AI a differentiator or table stakes
- **Challenges**: Data quality, model governance, skills gaps, ethics
- **Examples**: Specific AI applications creating value

### Emerging Technologies
- Other technologies gaining traction (IoT, blockchain, edge computing, etc.)
- How they complement cloud/SaaS/AI strategies
- Timeline to mainstream adoption

### Technology Investment Priorities
- Where companies are allocating IT budget
- Build vs buy decisions
- Technology stack modernization roadmaps

---

## 6. STRATEGIC IMPERATIVES & COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS

**Critical Success Factors:**
- What separates winners from losers in this industry
- Must-have capabilities vs differentiators

**Competitive Landscape:**
- Competitive intensity (low, medium, high)
- Basis of competition (cost, quality, innovation, service, speed)
- New entrants and disruptors

**Partnership & Ecosystem Trends:**
- M&A activity and consolidation
- Strategic partnerships and alliances
- Build vs buy vs partner decisions

**Customer Expectations:**
- Evolving customer demands
- Digital experience expectations
- Self-service and automation requirements

---

## 7. SOURCES & REFERENCES

Provide 10-15 authoritative sources for further research, organized by category:

**Industry Research & Market Intelligence:**
- Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, McKinsey, etc.)
- Industry association publications
- Market sizing and forecasting reports

**Regulatory & Compliance:**
- Regulatory body websites and guidance
- Key legislation and standards
- Compliance framework documentation

**Technology & Innovation:**
- Technology trend reports and surveys
- Cloud/SaaS adoption studies
- AI use case databases or case studies

**News & Analysis:**
- Leading industry publications
- Trade journals and newsletters
- Relevant podcasts or thought leadership

**Company & Competitive Intelligence:**
- Public company reports and investor presentations
- Industry benchmarking studies
- Case studies of successful implementations

For each source, include:
- **Title/Name**
- **URL** (where applicable)
- **Description**: What it covers (1-2 sentences)
- **Relevance**: Which section(s) of this strategy map it supports

---

## FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS:

- Use clear markdown headers and hierarchy (##, ###, ####)
- Keep paragraphs concise (3-5 sentences)
- Use bullet points for lists and key facts
- Include specific data points and metrics where possible
- Make it scannable - executives should be able to skim quickly
- Aim for 8-12 pages of dense, valuable content
- Be specific and actionable - avoid generic platitudes
- Include examples and real-world applications
- Focus on what's actually happening in the industry, not theoretical frameworks

## VERIFICATION & SOURCING REQUIREMENTS:

**CRITICAL: Only include verified, factual information with sources.**

- **All data points, statistics, and market figures MUST be sourced**
  - Include inline citations where facts are stated (e.g., "Market expected to reach $X billion by 2027 [Source: Gartner 2024]")
  - No unsourced claims about market size, growth rates, or adoption percentages
  
- **Company examples and case studies MUST be real and verifiable**
  - Only cite actual implementations you can reference
  - Include source where the example was documented (press release, case study, news article)
  
- **Technology adoption claims MUST be backed by surveys or studies**
  - Reference specific reports (e.g., "73% of enterprises using multi-cloud [Source: Flexera 2024 State of the Cloud Report]")
  - Cite analyst research, vendor surveys, or academic studies
  
- **Regulatory information MUST be accurate and current**
  - Link to official regulatory body sources
  - Include effective dates for new regulations
  - Verify compliance requirements are actual legal obligations
  
- **When information cannot be verified:**
  - Use qualifiers like "industry observers suggest" or "early indicators show"
  - Be transparent about uncertainty
  - Note in the sources section which areas need additional research
  
- **Source quality hierarchy** (prefer in this order):
  1. Primary sources (regulatory bodies, official reports, company filings)
  2. Tier-1 analyst firms (Gartner, Forrester, McKinsey, IDC)
  3. Industry associations and trade bodies
  4. Reputable business press (WSJ, FT, Bloomberg, Reuters)
  5. Specialized industry publications

**Do NOT include:**
- Speculative or unverified market predictions
- Anecdotal claims without documentation
- Statistics without clear source attribution
- "Common knowledge" that cannot be verified
- Outdated information presented as current

---

## CRITICAL: TECHNOLOGY SECTION DEPTH

The Technology Innovation & Digital Transformation section should be the most detailed part of this report. This is where strategic value is created.

For cloud/SaaS/AI, go beyond surface-level descriptions:
- Explain **how** these technologies drive revenue or reduce cost
- Provide **specific use cases** that are working in the industry
- Quantify impact where possible (e.g., "AI-powered pricing optimization increasing revenue by 5-15%")
- Identify which technologies are **differentiators** vs **table stakes**
- Note integration patterns (how cloud, SaaS, and AI work together)

---

## CRITICAL: AI/ML Use-Case Verification
When describing AI applications:
- DO: Cite specific company implementations with sources
- DO: Reference industry surveys showing adoption rates
- DON'T: Describe theoretical use-cases without evidence
- DON'T: Claim "most companies are using AI for X" without data

Please create a comprehensive, executive-ready document that would be valuable for strategic decision-making, sales enablement, or competitive intelligence.
