# Meeting Intelligence Report

Analyze the call recordings and generate a structured intelligence report.

## Input Requirements
- Audio/transcript from NotebookLM sources
- Focus on verified statements only - cite timestamps where possible

## Output Structure

### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (3-4 sentences)
- Meeting purpose and outcome
- Overall sentiment and engagement level
- Key decision or progression made

### 2. ATTENDEES & ENGAGEMENT
For each participant:
- Name, title, role in buying process
- Engagement level: Champion | Supportive | Neutral | Skeptical | Blocker
- Key quotes or behaviors demonstrating their position
- Confidence rating (High/Medium/Low) for each assessment

### 3. BUSINESS INTELLIGENCE CAPTURED
- Specific pain points mentioned (with context)
- Buying signals detected (urgency, budget hints, timeline pressures)
- Technical requirements discussed (integrations, compliance, features)
- Current tools/competitors mentioned and sentiment about them
- Success metrics or KPIs referenced

### 4. AGREED NEXT STEPS
- Committed actions with owners and deadlines
- Resources/documentation to provide
- Follow-up meetings scheduled or proposed

### 5. MEDDPICC GAP ANALYSIS
What we learned vs. what's still missing across:
- Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition

### 6. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
Priority actions beyond what was agreed:
- Stakeholders to engage 1:1 (and why)
- Information gaps to research
- Internal resources to involve
- Risk mitigation steps

### 7. INDIVIDUAL FOLLOW-UP STRATEGY
For each key attendee, provide:
- **Topics to explore**: Specific areas to dig deeper based on their comments
- **Question funnel**: 3-5 open-ended questions progressing from broad to specific, designed to uncover needs/concerns/influence

### 8. RISK FLAGS
- Missing stakeholders or decision-makers
- Misaligned expectations or objections raised
- Timeline/budget/process concerns
- Competitive threats identified

## Quality Standards
- Source all claims with timestamps or direct quotes
- Flag assumptions explicitly when confidence is low
- Prioritize actionable intelligence over generic observations
- Use UK business terminology and context
